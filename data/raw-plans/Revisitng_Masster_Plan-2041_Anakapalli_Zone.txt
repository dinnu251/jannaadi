The DRAFT MASTER PLAN FOR VISAKHAPATNAM METROPOLITAN REGION (VMR) outlines the PROPOSED LANDUSE for Vizag and its surrounding areas, providing a detailed developmental blueprint. This extensive plan spans a significant geographical area, bordered by Kakinada District to the northeast, Anakapalli District to the west, and Visakhapatnam District encompassing the central and eastern parts, with the Bay of Bengal forming the southern coastline. The VMR boundary is explicitly marked, alongside district and Mandal boundaries, delineating administrative and planning zones for future growth.

Key landmarks and localities prominently featured or implied by concentrated development include the bustling urban core of Visakhapatnam City, the industrial hub of Gajuwaka in the southwest, and the rapidly developing regions of Madhurawada and Rushikonda towards the northeast, known for their educational institutions and IT sector growth. The plan also covers the Anakapalli Zone, indicating its integration into the metropolitan development framework. Clusters representing rural settlements are distributed throughout the region, particularly along transport corridors and outside the dense urban centers.

The infrastructure scope is comprehensive, covering a vast array of facilities. The plan details an extensive road network, including existing and proposed National Highways (NH), State Highways (SH), Major District Roads (MDR), and various other roads or link roads essential for connectivity. Railway lines, both existing and proposed, are depicted, alongside symbols for major railway stations and bus terminals. The blueprint identifies locations for a port and an airport, crucial for regional connectivity and economic activity.

Land use categories are meticulously defined through a color-coded legend. This includes varying densities of residential zones (high, medium, low), extensive commercial areas, and designated industrial zones. Significant portions are allocated for public and semi-public use, encompassing educational institutions, hospitals, police stations, and fire stations. The plan emphasizes green infrastructure with recreational areas, open spaces, and large tracts of forest and hill areas. Agricultural lands and diverse water bodies, including rivers, tanks, canals, and the sea, are also clearly demarcated, alongside sites for Special Development Zones (SDZ).

Critical utilities and civic amenities are integral to the plan. Locations for Water Treatment Plants (WTP) and Sewage Treatment Plants (STP) are identified, ensuring sustainable water supply and waste management. Power substations are marked to indicate energy infrastructure development. The plan also designates Solid Waste Management (SWM) sites, reflecting a holistic approach to urban planning and environmental sustainability for the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region. The overall project, titled "DRAFT MASTER PLAN FOR VISAKHAPATNAM METROPOLITAN REGION PROPOSED LANDUSE," serves as a guiding document for future development in Vizag.
