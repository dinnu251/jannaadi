The DRAFT MASTER PLAN FOR VISAKHAPATNAM METROPOLITAN REGION (PROPOSED LANDUSE - 2041) outlines the comprehensive development strategy for the Visakhapatnam City Zone, projecting growth and infrastructure until 2041. This extensive blueprint covers areas within the Visakhapatnam District and extends into parts of Anakapalli District. Key geographic boundaries delineated include the VMR Boundary, District Boundary, Mandal Boundary, Village Boundary, Municipal Boundary, T.S. Ward Boundary, T.S. Block Boundary, and Survey Boundary. The plan also defines the Bhogapuram Airport & Influence Area (BAIA) Boundary and its designated Use Zone. The area of the Greater Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation (GVMC) is prominently depicted within the broader jurisdiction of the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region Development Authority (VMRDA).

Specific localities, mandals, and villages covered in this detailed plan include Sabbavaram Mandal, Gajuwaka Mandal, Pedagantyada Mandal, Mulagada Mandal, Pendurthi Mandal, Gopalapatnam Mandal, Maharani Peta Mandal, Visakhapatnam Rural Mandal, Anandapuram Mandal, and Thammadhar Mandal. Numerous individual locations are depicted such as Gollalapalem, Jaggarajupeta, Tungalam, Narava, Jerripothulapalem, Pedagadi, Venkatapatirajupea, Akkireddipalem, Tokada, Mindi, Kinagantyada, Pedagantyada, Mulagada, Perlupalem, Gopala, Lakshmi Puram, Cheemalapalle, Gandigundam, Pulagalipalem, Chinamushidivada, Valimeraka, Sowbhagya, Rayapuram, Krishnarayapuram, Rurushottapuram, Mokhasa, Purushottapuram, Vepagunta, Marri, Viziaramporam, Dabbanda, Mudasarlova, Adivivaram, Santapalem, Bakkannapalem, Pothinamallayyapalem, Mamidilova, Sontyam, Kommadi, Pedagadila, Yendada, and Gudlavanipalem. The plan incorporates the coastal region along the Bay of Bengal, featuring locations like Yarada and the prominent Dolphin Nose.

Infrastructure scopes detailed within the plan encompass various proposed land use zones including Residential Use, Mixed Use Zone-2BA, Industrial Use Zone, Proposed Industrial Use, Proposed PSP (Public & Semi-Public) Use Zone, and Proposed Recreational Use Zone. Existing infrastructure highlighted comprises a network of National Highways (NH), State Highways (SH), Major District Roads (MDR), Other District Roads (ODR), Village Roads, Railway Lines, HT Power lines, Existing BRTS (Bus Rapid Transit System) Roads, airports, existing religious facilities, fish landing sites, HT Pylons, railway stations, existing recreational playgrounds, and sea ports. Significant proposed transportation networks include Existing Roads Proposed for Widening, Proposed BRTS corridors, Proposed Suburban Railway lines, Proposed Metro Rail lines, Proposed New Railway lines, and Elevated Corridors, signifying a robust future transportation system.

Key landmarks explicitly shown or referenced on the map include Gajuwaka, Mulagada, Dolphin Nose, and Myraki Forest, with Madhurawada referenced as part of the "Myraki Forest Madhurawada" existing recreational playground. The master plan for the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region, developed by VMRDA and involving the Greater Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation (GVMC), targets a comprehensive urban and regional transformation by 2041. While specific developmental budgets are not itemized on this blueprint, the extensive scope across various infrastructure and land use sectors implies substantial investment for the future growth of Visakhapatnam.
