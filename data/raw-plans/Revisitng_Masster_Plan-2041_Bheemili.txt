The Draft Master Plan for the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region (VMRDA), titled "PROPOSED LANDUSE (Rev-1)," outlines extensive developmental strategies for the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region. This comprehensive plan covers significant areas within the Visakhapatnam District and extends northward into parts of the Vizianagaram District. The regional boundary is prominently defined by the Bay of Bengal to the south and east, stretching along the coastline from Pudimadaka in the southwest to beyond Bhogapuram in the northeast. Key districts involved are Visakhapatnam District and Vizianagaram District, establishing a broad geographical scope for future growth and infrastructure development under the VMRDA.

The master plan explicitly identifies and plans for major urban and suburban centers including Visakhapatnam city itself, along with important growth nodes like Madhurawada, Rushikonda, and Gajuwaka. Other significant localities and villages depicted or directly impacted by the proposed land use include Bheemunipatnam, Anakapalle, Anandapuram, Pendurthi, Kothavalasa, Sabbavaram, Marripalem, Chinamushidivada, Yellapuvanipalem, Parawada, Desapatrunipalem, Revupolavaram, Akkireddipalem, Jaggayyapalem, Vedullapadu, Chintala-agraharam, and Pudimadaka. In the northern extension into Vizianagaram District, areas like Bhogapuram, Ravada, Chintapalli, and Maradam are also part of the planning landscape, indicating future expansion and connectivity across the regional boundaries.

The VMRDA's Proposed Landuse map details a wide array of infrastructure and land use categories crucial for urban development. This includes the planning and upgrade of major roads, existing roads, and proposed road networks to enhance connectivity. Extensive areas are allocated for various land uses: residential (existing and proposed), commercial (existing and proposed), and industrial (existing and proposed, notably around Gajuwaka). Significant emphasis is placed on public and semi-public facilities, green areas, parks, open spaces, and bio-conservation zones, with large sections dedicated to forest land and protecting existing green cover. The plan also addresses critical utilities such as waste disposal sites (solid waste management), and implicitly includes water supply infrastructure given the presence of water bodies and urban development zones. Transport and communication infrastructure are central, featuring the Visakhapatnam Airport, Visakhapatnam Port, existing railway lines, and planning for enhanced port and airport connectivity. The Gosthani River and other existing water bodies are integral to the environmental and hydrological planning of the region.

This "DRAFT MASTER PLAN FOR VISAKHAPATNAM METROPOLITAN REGION" by VMRDA is a foundational document for future projects and zoning. While specific budgets are not detailed on this map, it provides the framework for all subsequent developmental funding and departmental actions. Key landmarks prominently featured include the Visakhapatnam Airport, Visakhapatnam Port, the industrial and commercial hub of Gajuwaka, the IT and residential hub of Madhurawada, the scenic coastal area of Rushikonda, and the historic town of Bheemunipatnam. The plan also designates special development zones, tourism zones, and heritage zones, aiming for balanced growth that encompasses economic, environmental, and cultural aspects. The proposed land use also accounts for agricultural areas, ensuring a comprehensive approach to land management and resource allocation across the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region.
